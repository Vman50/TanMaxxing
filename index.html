How TanMaxxing calculates

Live UV. Real hourly forecasts from Open-Meteo (major weather models) for your coordinates, or OpenUV with a key. The curve is the real forecast, with night shaded between sunset and sunrise.

Burn time. UV Index means erythemal irradiance = UVI × 0.025 W/m² (CIE/WHO). Time to redness ≈ MED ÷ (1.5 × UVI) min, where MED is your skin's minimal erythemal dose.

MED (J/m², approx): I 200 · II 250 · III 350 · IV 450 · V 600 · VI 1000.

Sunscreen / oil. Your product's SPF multiplies burn time, applied conservatively (~half label, since real coverage is thin) so the safe number errs short.

Glow. Each session adds UV dose (UVI × min × 0.015 SED) to a melanin stimulus that saturates toward your type's ceiling and fades on an ~18-day half-life.

Models are estimates; individual skin varies. Not medical advice.
